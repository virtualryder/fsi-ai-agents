# ROI Analysis: Wealth & RM Copilot Agent
## Business Case for AI-Powered Relationship Manager Productivity

---

## Executive Summary

A wealth management firm with 50 relationship managers, each managing an average of 80 client relationships, can expect **$3.2M–$3.8M in annual productivity value** from deploying the Wealth & RM Copilot Agent. Unlike cost-reduction-only ROI cases, this agent's benefit is dual-sided: it recovers 10+ hours per week of RM capacity currently consumed by administrative tasks (cost reduction) while simultaneously enabling RMs to deepen client engagement and grow AUM (revenue opportunity). Payback occurs within **8–12 weeks** of go-live.

### The Three Productivity Killers

**1. Meeting preparation (2–3 hours per client meeting):**
An RM managing 80 relationships, each meeting quarterly, prepares for approximately 20 client meetings per month. At 2.5 hours of preparation each — pulling portfolio performance, market context, product ideas, and regulatory documentation — that is 50 hours per month, or 25% of total RM time, on meeting prep alone.

**2. Proposal and documentation writing (3–6 hours per proposal):**
An investment proposal — even a simple rebalancing recommendation — requires: client profile review, product analysis, cost comparison, suitability rationale, Reg BI disclosure language, and a client-appropriate narrative. Compliance standards have increased this burden 40% since 2020.

**3. Reactive compliance tasks (1–2 hours per incident):**
New product approvals, account restriction reviews, compliance questionnaire responses, and KYC update requests interrupt RM workflow unpredictably, consuming focus time that is worth more to the firm in client-facing activity.

## 11. Suite Compounding Effect

Agent 05 + Agent 03 + Agent 01 compound ROI:

- Agent 03 (KYC/CDD) surfaces client risk tier and document status to Agent 05 — the RM receives a clean, current KYC summary without requesting it manually
- Agent 01 (Financial Crime Investigation) SAR events do not surface to Agent 05 (architectural enforcement), but Agent 03's risk tier change — which may follow an SAR event — does surface, prompting the RM to update the client relationship with appropriate additional diligence (no tipping off preserved)
- Combined value for a firm running Agents 03 + 05: **~$7.2M annually** for a 50-RM / 10,000-client-book institution

## 12. Intangible Benefits

1. **RM recruitment and retention:** Top wealth management professionals choose employers who give them technology leverage. An AI copilot that eliminates administrative burden and amplifies client-facing capacity is a recruitable differentiator — particularly among younger RMs who expect AI in their workflow.

2. **Compliance confidence:** RMs who use the copilot's compliance review node operate with confidence that their proposals meet Reg BI requirements before they reach clients. This reduces anxiety-driven over-caution (recommending only the safest options out of compliance fear) and enables more tailored, best-interest recommendations.

3. **Knowledge transfer:** When an RM leaves, their client relationships, proposal history, and engagement patterns are preserved in the system. The next RM assigned to the book has full context from day one — reducing the client attrition risk that normally follows RM transitions.

4. **Consistency at scale:** An RM with 80 clients can only be as thorough as time allows. The copilot ensures every client receives the same quality of preparation, portfolio analysis, and proposal documentation — regardless of where they fall in the RM's priority stack.

5. **Regulatory change absorption:** As Reg BI, FINRA rules, and ERISA requirements evolve, the compliance review node and suitability check are updated centrally — all RMs benefit from the update immediately without re-training. Compliance drift across a 50-RM team becomes a configuration management problem, not a training problem.
